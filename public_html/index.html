All My Technical Projects.

During my time at Texas A&M University I have completed 3 projects.

The single-cycle CPU project was completed using a combination of ARM assembly language, Verilog, and C language on Raspberry Pi 4 hardware. I developed a 32-bit single-cycle ARM processor. Each indivudal component (ALU, Sign Extender, Adders, Mux, etc.) was devleoped from scratch and then combined together to form the working CPU.

The client-server project was completed using C++. This project was a program that connected to a given server utilizing IPC mechanisms (Pipe, FIFO, MQ, Shared Memory). The program can transfer data, file messages, and create new channels. The server hosted electrocardiogram data points for patients. Client has access to retrieve the datapoints.

The machine learning project was completed using Python. The project was an image classification program that utilized machine learning to classify the type of fruit and whether it is rotten based on an input image of fruit. Three algorithms were used: CNN, SVM, and a combination of the two.

Service Project for Habitat for Humanity.

Aggie Habitat for Humanity is the Texas A&M Campus Chapter of Habitat for Humanity International and work closely with Bryan/College Station. Their mission is focusing on building homes, fundraising to sponsor a family;s home, advocating for affordable housing, and educating the Texas A&M campus and community about Habitat for Humanity’s mission. Every year they put on two major fundraising events; one of which is called Shack-a-Thon.

Shack-a-thon is an event where Aggie Habitat for Humanity auctions off spaces around Rudder Fountain to a bunch of different student organizations to build shacks! Students are allowed to tap in their creativity and showcase it while abiding by campus rules and the outlined conditions to ensure utmost safety for the students and those that are passing. This event in particular helps raise poverty housing awareness among the community of Texas A&M.

During my sophomore year I was a counselor in the freshman leadership organization called Freshmen Reaching Excellence in Engineering (F.R.E.E.). F.R.E.E. wanted to take part in this fundraising event to provide service for those in need of housing. The design of choice was Shaquille O'Neal purely because of the play-on words of Shaq and Shack. My committee and I, with the help of the rest of the organization, completed this project in a few weeks and put it on display in front of Kyle Field. The shack had interactive elements to help boost awareness such as a functional basketball hoop on the front, ping-pong table on the side, and corn-hole boards. Local news channels came by to televise the shack which in turn also boosted awareness for Aggie Habitat for Humanity’s mission.

I seek to do more for the community such as participating in fundraising events like Shack-a-Thon. It is not only a great opportunity to do good for the community, but also an opportunity to learn a lot while doing so.
